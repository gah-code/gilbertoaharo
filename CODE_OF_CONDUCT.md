# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We expect everyone who participates in this project to abide by this Code of Conduct.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful** — Treat all participants with respect and dignity
- **Being inclusive** — Welcome people of all backgrounds and identities
- **Being constructive** — Provide thoughtful, helpful feedback
- **Being open-minded** — Listen to different perspectives and ideas
- **Being professional** — Keep discussions focused and on-topic

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or exclusionary comments
- Personal attacks or insults
- Threats or intimidating behavior
- Unwelcome sexual or romantic advances
- Spam or off-topic trolling
- Publishing others' private information without consent

## Enforcement

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate corrective action in response to instances of unacceptable behavior.

Unacceptable behavior may result in:
- A warning
- Temporary or permanent ban from the project
- Reported to GitHub for policy violations

## Reporting Issues

If you witness or experience unacceptable behavior, please report it by:

1. **For minor issues:** Open an issue or comment privately
2. **For serious violations:** Email the maintainers directly or report to GitHub

All reports will be handled confidentially and investigated promptly.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All project spaces (issues, PRs, discussions, code)
- Community spaces that represent the project
- Individual communications related to project business
